2. DISCLOSURE OF SPECIFIC HAZARDS: TOXICOLOGICAL AND THERMAL

Participation in resin art classes involves inherent and obvious risks associated with chemical handling and high-temperature tools. These include:

  • Toxicological Risks: Exposure to liquid epoxy resin components and chemical pigments can cause contact dermatitis, skin sensitisation, and respiratory irritation.
  • High-Temperature Thermal Hazards: Industrial heat guns used to remove bubbles and manipulate resin reach temperatures of 600°C, posing a risk of severe thermal burns upon contact.
  • Exothermic Curing Reactions: The chemical hardening process of resin is an "exothermic reaction," generating significant internal heat. Improper handling of bulk resin during the curing phase can result in burns or smoke.

5. PRODUCT OUTCOME AND PROPERTY DAMAGE DISCLAIMER

Resin art is a handmade, chemical medium. You acknowledge the following:

  1. Aesthetic Characteristics: Finished products may contain bubbles, dust, or natural blemishes. These are inherent to the handmade process and are not considered defects.
  2. UV Sensitivity: While UV-resistant resins are used, cured resin remains UV sensitive and may yellow over time if exposed to direct sunlight.
  3. Thermal Threshold: Cured resin is not suitable for extreme heat. Do not place heat sources exceeding 50°C on any resin-coated item (e.g., hot coffee mugs or dishes), as this may cause surface deformation or chemical leaching. Resin is only food-safe up to this 50°C limit.
